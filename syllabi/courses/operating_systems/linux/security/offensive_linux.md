# Offensive `Linux`

## Credits
Mark Veltzer [mark.veltzer@gmail.com](mailto:mark.veltzer@gmail.com)

## Description
This intensive course is designed for experienced penetration testers seeking to enhance their offensive capabilities in `Linux` environments. The course covers advanced exploitation techniques, privilege escalation methods, persistence mechanisms, and lateral movement strategies. Students will develop practical skills in exploiting `Linux` systems through hands-on scenarios and real-world attack simulations, preparing them for complex penetration testing engagements.

## Duration
40 hours / 5 days

## Intended Audience
* Experienced Penetration Testers
* Red Team Operators
* Security Researchers
* Ethical Hackers with `Linux` experience

## Prerequisites
* Strong foundational knowledge of `Linux` operating systems
* Experience with penetration testing methodologies
* Understanding of networking concepts and protocols
* Familiarity with command-line tools and scripting
* Basic knowledge of exploit development concepts

## Objectives
* Perform advanced reconnaissance and fingerprinting of `Linux` systems
* Identify and exploit privilege escalation vulnerabilities
* Develop and implement persistence mechanisms in `Linux` environments
* Execute lateral movement and credential harvesting techniques
* Exploit common `Linux` services and misconfigurations
* Combine multiple attack vectors in complex, multi-stage scenarios
* Apply evasion techniques to avoid detection during engagements
* Conduct comprehensive offensive operations in `Linux` environments

## Exercises
* Practical exploitation exercises in controlled environments
* Multi-stage attack scenario simulations
* CTF-style challenges and problem-solving
* Custom script development for automated attacks
* Real-world penetration testing scenarios
