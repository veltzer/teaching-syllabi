# Data Driven Project Management

## Credits
Mark Veltzer [mark.veltzer@gmail.com](mailto:mark.veltzer@gmail.com)

## Description
This course is introduces how to manage a project using a data driven approach. This approach
which has been promulgated by successful corporations such as Google and Apple has shown
great success being used from the startup level where it is critical since resources are limited,
through the enterprise level and up to the corporate level.

## Duration
24 hours / 3 days

## Intended Audience
* Product managers who have not used a data driven approach for decision making and would like
to understand both the theory and the practical aspects of the approach.
* Product managers who have a non formal knowledge of this approach and would like to get a
more complete understanding of the approach and current tools in the space.

## Prerequisites
Any of the following
* Experience in product management
* Experience in project management
* Experience in team leading
* Experience in tech management
* Experience in account management in tech.

## Objectives
* understand the core concepts and principles of Data Driven Project Management
* gain practical knowledge of The Data Drive approach (4 hours)
* gain practical knowledge of Industry case studies and examples (2 hours)
* gain practical knowledge of Frameworks (4 hours)

## Outline
* The Data Drive approach (4 hours)
    * What is it?
    * Marketing decisions
    * Business decisions
    * Understanding your users
    * New products
    * Features
    * UI
    * Terminating products, features or UI elements.
    * Further Data collection
    * Exercise
        * Analyze the products you are involved with and list data driven
        decisions you can make about it and their advantages for your
        organization.
* Industry case studies and examples (2 hours)
    * Google search
    * Twiggle search metrics
    * Gmail
    * Exercise
        * Find examples of industry use of data driven and explain it.
* Frameworks (4 hours)
    * Why do you need a data and analytics framework?
    * The 4 aspects of the data and analytics framework
    * examples of frameworks
        * AARRR    (web and product)
        * HEART (UI)
    * Creating your own framework
    * Exercises
        * Presented with AARRR data explain the data.
        * Given data for a business case what is the best framework to work with?
* BI introduction (4 hours)
    * REMARK: Do you need this chapter at all?
    * What is BI?
    * BI platforms.
    * Data visualization and dashboard design.
    * Measurement examples
        * Balanced Scorecard
        * Six Sigma
    * Exercise: Kibana dashboard design
        * Install kibana
        * import sample data
        * design your own dashboard in Kibana
* Tools (6 hours)
    * Web analytics
        * Google analytics
    * Behavioral (this is the focus)
        * Mixpanel
        * Amplitude
    * Power BI tools
        * Tableau
        * `Oracle` BI
    * Power search/big data tools
        * Elastic search + Kibana
        * `Splunk`
    * Rolling your own
        * Big data considerations
        * Advantages and disadvantages
    * Exercises:
        * Google analytics exercise (this exercise is removed)
            * create a site
            * adjust the site for google analytics
            * create fake user access logs
            * view google analytics panels.
        * Mixpanel exercise
            * exercise with your data.
        * Given data in a `MySQL` database, derive insights from the data using `SQL`.
    * REMARK: If you choose to exercise with your own data it is best to use
    a data set pre-prepared by the client and an exercise which was
    pre-prepared by the client to avoid wasting wasting time and/or
    a failed exercise.
    * REMARK: do you need the google analytics and `MySQL` exercises?
* Visualization (1 hour)
    * Which visualization to use for which type of data.
    * Choosing between several visualizations that fit a certain data type.
    * "Lying" and "Telling the truth" with data.
* Data (4 hours)
    * REMARK: do you need this chapter?
    * How does data driven relate to big data?
    * Data retention policies
    * Data collection policies
    * Privacy concerns
