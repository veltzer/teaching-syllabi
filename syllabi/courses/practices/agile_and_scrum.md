# Agile & Scrum for Developers

## Description
This course introduces developers to the Agile methodology and the Scrum framework. Participants
will learn how Scrum works in practice, including sprints, standups, retrospectives, and backlog
management. The course also covers `Kanban`, user stories, estimation techniques, and `agile` engineering
practices. By the end of the course, developers will understand how to work effectively within an
`agile` team and contribute to continuous improvement.

## Duration
8 hours / 1 day

## Intended Audience
* software developers working in or transitioning to `agile` teams.
* team leads who want to understand `agile` principles and apply them effectively.
* engineers who want to improve their collaboration and delivery practices.

## Prerequisites
* No special requirements for this course.
* Some experience working in a software development team is helpful.

## Objectives
* understand the core principles of the Agile manifesto
* learn the Scrum framework and its roles, events, and artifacts
* gain practical knowledge of running sprints, standups, and retrospectives
* understand `Kanban` and how it complements Scrum
* learn how to write effective user stories and estimate work
* explore `agile` engineering practices that support iterative delivery
