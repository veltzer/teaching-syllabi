# Technical Leadership

## Description
Technical leadership is what happens after you stop being measured purely on the code you ship. The staff and
principal engineer roles, the tech lead role, and the architect role all require a different set of skills:
shaping technical strategy, influencing without authority, making decisions that span multiple teams,
mentoring at scale, and being the engineer that other engineers want to ask for help.

This five day course is a practical treatment of technical leadership for engineers who are stepping into or
already operating at staff-and-above levels. It covers the staff-engineer archetypes, technical strategy and
roadmaps, design review and architectural review boards, decision-making with imperfect information, building
and maintaining technical credibility, mentoring and growing other engineers, navigating organizational
politics without becoming political, and the day-to-day calendar of a technical leader. The course is grounded
in the work of `Will Larson`, `Tanya Reilly`, `Camille Fournier` and the public engineering blogs of
high-functioning organizations.

## Duration
40 hours / 5 days

## Intended Audience
* senior developers stepping into staff/principal/tech-lead roles
* tech leads and architects who want to be more effective
* engineering managers who lead technical leaders
* engineers preparing for staff-engineer interviews

## Prerequisites
* several years of senior software development experience
* experience leading at least one non-trivial technical project
* basic familiarity with software architecture and team dynamics

## Objectives
* describe the staff-engineer archetypes and pick the one that fits you
* shape and communicate technical strategy
* run effective design reviews and architecture decisions
* mentor engineers from junior through senior
* influence without organizational authority
* manage your own time, focus and growth as a technical leader
* build the credibility that makes the `rest` possible
